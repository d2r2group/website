Principal Investigator


p.schindler@NEU

Dr. Peter Schindler

Assistant Professor at Northeastern University, MIE

Ph.D. in Physics, 2015 (University of Vienna)
M.Sc. in Physics, 2011 (University of Vienna)

Bio

Dr. Peter Schindler is leading the Data-Driven Renewables Research (D2R2) group that seeks to discover novel materials for renewable energy applications using high-throughput, quantum chemistry calculations, and data-driven materials property predictions. Prior to joining Northeastern he was a senior scientist at Aionics, a startup that has become a recognized leader in the field of battery informatics, by co-innovating with battery manufacturers through AI and high-performance computing. During his postdoctoral research at the University of Vienna and Stanford University, his research established renewable energy materials both computationally and experimentally, for which he was awarded the Erwin-Schrödinger fellowship by the Austrian Science Fund (FWF). He received his Ph.D. in physics from the University of Vienna working on semiconductor synthesis and characterization. His expertise in both experimental synthesis and computational materials simulations enables him to carry out cutting-edge research at the intersection of the two fields.

Research Interests

Materials Informatics, AI, Ab-initio Modeling, Materials Discovery, Renewable Energy Materials, Surfaces, Heterogeneous Catalysis

Fun Facts

Despite being originally from Austria, he neither is a good skier nor plays a classical instrument. He does however dance Bachata and Kizomba and knows how to play the drums.

Postdoctoral Researchers


s.rezaei@NEU

Dr. S. Emad Rezaei

Postdoctoral Research Associate at Northeastern University, MIE

Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering, 2022 (University of Virginia)
M.Sc. in Mechanical and Materials Engineering, 2018 (Wright State University)
B.Sc. in Materials Science and Engineering, 2015 (Sharif University of Technology)

Bio

Currently, he holds the role of a postdoctoral research associate at Northeastern University, specializing in the Data-Driven Renewable Research group. His core research revolves around conducting high-throughput Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations and the application of machine learning models to tackle quantum mechanics and materials science challenges. Prior to undertaking this postdoctoral position, he accomplished his PhD at the University of Virginia, where he specialized in Electrical Engineering and studied charge transport in materials using quantum mechanics methods. In the earlier stages of his academic journey, during his MSc, he delved into the enhancement of low-dimensional materials through molecular dynamics investigations.

Research Interests

Computational materials science, Materials Informatics, Data-Driven methods

Fun Facts

He ran a marathon without training!

Graduate Students


mehdizadeh.a@NEU

Ardavan Mehdizadeh

Ph.D. Student in Mechanical Engineering

B.Sc. in Civil Engineering, 2015 (Sharif University of Technology)

Bio

Born and raised in Tehran, Iran, I began a journey of scientific exploration; with a B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from Sharif University of Technology as part of my qualifications, I entered the world of molecular dynamics and the intricacies of protein interactions.
My research journey took me to various research institutes within and beyond Sharif University. I deeply engaged in Molecular Dynamics simulations, uncovering the complications of protein-protein and protein-nanoparticle interactions. I also had the interest to investigate the complexities of protein folding and the prediction of protein structure and function.
Now, I’m eagerly diving into the exciting world of computational material science, focusing primarily on quantum chemistry calculations and data-driven approaches, including machine learning methods, for my PhD. This integration of sciences promises groundbreaking discoveries in machine learning models driven by statistical insights, and I can’t wait to explore it further.

Research Interests

Computational Materials Science, Computational Quantum Chemistry, Multiscale Modeling, Molecular Dynamics, Density Functional Theory (DFT), Data-Driven Methods, Machine Learning
