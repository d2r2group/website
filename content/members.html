Principal Investigator


Dr. Peter Schindler

p.schindler@NEU

Assistant Professor at Northeastern University, MIE

Ph.D. in Physics, 2015 (University of Vienna)
M.Sc. in Physics, 2011 (University of Vienna)

Bio

Dr. Peter Schindler is leading the Data-Driven Renewables Research (D2R2) group that seeks to discover novel materials for renewable energy applications using high-throughput, quantum chemistry calculations, and data-driven materials property predictions. He is a scientific advisory board member (and former senior scientist) at Aionics Inc., a startup that has become a recognized leader in the field of battery informatics, by co-innovating with battery manufacturers through AI and high-performance computing. During his postdoctoral research at the University of Vienna and Stanford University, his research established renewable energy materials both computationally and experimentally, for which he was awarded the Erwin-Schrödinger fellowship by the Austrian Science Fund (FWF). He received his Ph.D. in physics from the University of Vienna working on thin-film semiconductor synthesis and characterization. His expertise in both experimental synthesis and computational materials simulations enables him to carry out cutting-edge research at the intersection of the two fields.

Research Interests

Materials Informatics, AI, Ab-initio Modeling, Materials Discovery, Renewable Energy Materials, Surfaces, Heterogeneous Catalysis

Fun Facts

Despite being originally from Austria, he neither is a good skier nor plays a classical instrument. He does however dance Bachata and Kizomba and knows how to play the drums. He is also into street photography.

Ph.D. Students


Ardavan Mehdizadeh

mehdizadeh.a@NEU

Ph.D. Student in Mechanical Engineering

B.Sc. in Civil Engineering, 2015 (Sharif University of Technology)

Bio

Born and raised in Tehran, Iran, Ardavan began his scientific journey with a B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from Sharif University of Technology, where he explored molecular dynamics and protein interactions. His research led him to various institutes, where he engaged in molecular dynamics simulations, focusing on protein-protein and protein-nanoparticle interactions, as well as protein folding and structure prediction.
Currently, Ardavan is pursuing a PhD in computational materials science. His research combines quantum chemistry and data-driven methods, particularly machine learning, to address complex problems in surface chemistry. His work centers on surface reconstruction, with the goal of predicting atomic rearrangements on surfaces using statistical techniques and neural network force fields (NFFs). Ardavan aims to develop a universal NNF capable of modeling surface reconstructions across a variety of materials. By integrating principles from physics, materials science, and computer science, he seeks to advance the application of machine learning in surface science.

Research Interests

Computational Materials Science, Computational Chemistry, Density Functional Theory (DFT), Molecular Dynamics, Data-Driven Methods, Machine Learning

Luca Reichert

Ph.D. Student at Technical University of Munich (TUM)

M. Eng. in Material Science, 2021 (University of Koblenz, Germany)
B. Eng. in Material Science, 2021 (University of Applied Science, Koblenz, Germany)

Bio

Never meaning to start a scientific career, my journey started with an apprenticeship as a materials tester at ThyssenKrupp, one of the biggest steel producers in Germany. In my second year, I got used to the work of our engineers and realized that this is the work I wanted to do in my future career, both research and project management.
To reach this goal, I studied Material Science with main focus on ceramics and high-temperature materials. During this time, I worked as a research assistant at an institute near my university and in parallel as a teaching assistant. Discovering that the combination of research and teaching is my passion, I made the decision to begin the path to becoming a professor.
After a quick step back in the industry at MTU Aero Engines in Munich for 1 ½ years, I recently started my doctorate at the Technical University of Munich. Here, I am currently investigating catalytic materials for the Oxygen Reduction Reaction in PEM-Fuel Cells.

Research Interests

Crystallography and Crystal Chemistry, Density Functional Theory (DFT), Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD), Theoretical and Physical Chemistry, (Computational) Material Science
